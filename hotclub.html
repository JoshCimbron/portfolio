The Hotclub

The Hotclub is a popular waterfront bar dealing with the challenges of the Coronavirus Pandemic. This website redesign introduces new features that allows customers to make reservations, order food online, and find relevant information quickly.

Project Type

Personal design challenge

Time Frame

April - May 2020

Role

Research, information architecture, wireframing, visual design, prototyping and testing

The Problem

The Hotclub has a website that is rarely updated and provides a poor first impression for new customers. Guests currently rely on multiple social media platforms or must call the Hotclub for information about the establishment. Additionally, the Coronavirus Pandemic has introduced strict new restrictions to the food industry that impacts dine in service.

User Research

To jumpstart my research I had to figure out what content and information needed to be on the website. I conducted guerilla research, qualitative interviews, and a comparitive analysis to better understand what our users needed.

Comparative Analysis

I explored a variety of different restaurant websites ranging from small family owned businesses to large chains. I took a look at industry statistics to develop a bigger picture of the problem.

Qualitative Interviews

I conducted interviews with the owners to learn more about their marketing strategies and their vision for the future of the business.

Guerilla Research

As an employee of the Hotclub I was able to interact and emphasize with our customers. It’s what drove me to start a redesign in the first place. I took note of the frequently asked questions asked by our customers over the phone.

Ideation

I organized my findings in to an affinity diagram. Similarities in the data were grouped together to determine who our users are and their needs. I used these insights to guide a series of quick, low fidelity wireframes to create variations of certain features and map out the information architecture.

Visual Design

After a series of low fidelity concepts I selected the strongest one. I developed a style guide and created a high fidelity visual design and prototype.

Homepage

Most of the content can be found on the homepage. The most important content (like reservations, online ordering, and contact info) is placed towards the top of the page. The “Our Story” section is placed on a different page for users who feel like exploring the Hotclub’s history.

Our Story

This space highlights the Hotclub’s long history in the Providence community. The Hotclub isn’t just a bar. It’s a culmination of the memories built by many different people over the decades.

User Testing

A small user base tested the prototype and a second iteration was developed based on their feedback.

View the prototype

Results

Unfortunately, the owners already hired someone to build a new website while I wrapped up this unsolicited design. If I had the opportunity to continue this project the next step would be to monitor the current health restrictions and work closely with management to make the appropriate changes to the website.
